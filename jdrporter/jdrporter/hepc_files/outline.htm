HEPATITIS C PREVALENCE
1.8% of U.S. population infected
85% chronic infection,
20% of these develop cirrhosis of liver,
1-5% develop liver cancer
20-30 years from infection to end stage disease
HCV AND DRUG INJECTORS
IDUs 60% of new cases in U.S. population
IDUs 20-59% of chronic infections
HCV prevalence between 65-90% among IDUs
With less than two years injection, 60% IDUs are HCV
Cookers and cotton 13% of HCV transmission among IDUs

KNOWLEDGE OF HCV
CONCLUSIONS
Widespread knowledge that HCV is a disease affecting drug injectors.
Some have basic information that HCV affects the liver, is potentially fatal, and transmitted by syringe sharing. 25% have no correct information.
There is confusion and inaccuracy about types of hepatitis, transmission, symptoms, effects, prevention, testing.
Less knowledge of HCV than of HIV; few knew if they were tested or knew their HCV status.
CONCLUSIONS (continued)
Those who are most knowledgeable are:
HCV+
Whites
In drug treatment.
Among those not in drug treatment, regular clients of needle exchange programs.
STRATEGIES FOR PREVENTION
Low literacy and Spanish pamphlets.
Presentations and educational material in venues where injectors congregate.
Incorporation of HCV education in community-based HIV prevention.
Testing and counseling in drug treatment and syringe exchanges.
Availability of sterile injection equipment.
