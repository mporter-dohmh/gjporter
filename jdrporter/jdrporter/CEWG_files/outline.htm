STAFF COMMENTS:
 CLIENT ANGER
There was a heightened level of anger. Our clients are in a vulnerable state and their emotions are augmented, enhanced.
CLIENT COMMENTS: ANGER
It got me angry, and not the sheer thing that they would come into our backyard and slap us in the face. What got me angry was I felt the government had known about this and done nothing with it. I was angry at the terrorists, but I was just as angry at the government.
CLIENT:
ANGER AT FOREIGNERS
It’s sad that we can’t get a small rice and gravy and a hug, and all these foreigners are coming in and opening up stores and the government helping them. It’s a crying shame. That’s why I never voted.
CLIENT ANGER:
GOVERNMENT POLICY
America is always the first to stick their noses in other people’s business, and we have started wars we really had no conflict with.
CLIENT FEAR: ANTHRAX
I wasn’t worried enough to stop doing it, but I was worried every time I mixed up some dope it was going to be anthrax.
CLIENT FEAR: SAFETY
I’m getting ready to go out of town and I’m worried somebody plant a bomb on the bus.
CLIENT: TRAUMA
My mother died when I was young and my father abused me when I was young and it just brought all them memories back.

Pattern I: Acute, Persistent Fear
The fear is always sitting there, sitting there waiting.
Pattern II: Moderate Fear
Whenever I catch a cab, I look and if it’s one of those types of people that’s driving the cab, I don’t get in.
VARIATIONS IN CURRENT RESPONSE
Pattern III: Mild Fear
It’s better not to think about it. Anything could happen anywhere, but I’m busy, I keep my mind occupied, I work.
Pattern IV: No Fear 
I have no concerns. It’s kind of abstract because it’s not here. I’ll leave it over there. It didn’t dry up the dope.
INCREASE IN BENZO USE
Around that time I was taking at least one or two a day and then one or two didn’t do anything and that’s when I started taking more to relax, calm down, because I’m a nervous wreck and it calms you.
EFFECT OF 9/11 ON DRUG SALES
No change in street sales of heroin or crack.
No change in availability of street drugs.
No change in price or purity.
More open use.
DRUG TREATMENT: STAFF
Methadone staff put more focus on 9/11 than did the inpatient program.
Inpatient staff was committed to the regular routine with a focus on recovery.
No increase in intakes for treatment.
DRUG TREATMENT: CLIENTS
No worry on 9/11 about getting to program
No change in the  time when medicated.
Great anxiety that clinic would be closed and methadone would not be available.
Client concerns about 9/11 were not fully expressed in treatment.
THERAPEUTIC TECHNIQUE
In both programs, clients were allowed to vent feelings and 9/11 was used as a spur to recovery.
No extra groups for clients about 9/11 in either program.
Methadone program relaxed medication time, inpatient program did not expand usual TV hours.
INPATIENT PROGRAM RESPONSE
   We don’t want to overload people by seeing the Twin Towers fall 4,000 times.
SUGGESTIONS FOR THE FUTURE: STAFF
Training for disaster response with plans for continuation of methadone dispensing.
Immediate staff meeting to share staff and client concerns.
Rigid federal guidelines are not needed.
SUGGESTIONS FOR THE FUTURE:  CLIENTS
Need for plan to dispense methadone in case of attack.
Counselors should raise issue in groups.
One-time voluntary group to discuss the events.
Voluntary groups on grief and anger.
CONCLUSIONS
Reactions similar to others, but more diffuse anger. Differences by gender, panic disorder, drug involvement.
For most, return to normal with lingering anxiety about future attacks.
Drug use behavior not change substantially
Street sales did not change.
CONCLUSIONS
Agreement between clients and staff in methadone program on most recollections.
Some difference in specific program response due to nature of the program.
Similarity in therapy technique, type of clients most affected, and need for future planning.
SUGGESTIONS FOR RESEARCH
Retrospective studies should include different groups.
Analysis of clinic-wide urine screens not helpful.
Study of different cities, types of programs with large random samples.
Must be done as soon as possible.
