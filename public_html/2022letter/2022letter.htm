Dear friends,

 

It has been our custom to write a year-end letter to share news of our family with the many friends whom we seldom see but still like to maintain connection with. You all know who you are.

 

In 2021, because of COVID our travels consisted of trips to medical appointments, pick ups at the grocery store, and at the drug store. In addition, we took walks in our neighborhood and attended meetings and lectures via Zoom.  2022 is basically the same with the following major additions: Jake’s Princeton graduation, Henry’s Bar Mitzvah, Thanksgiving, Prevention Point’s 30th Anniversary celebration, MAA Mathfest The Princeton Prize in Race Relation’s Philadelphia section award ceremony, retirement events at Bryn Mawr and restaurant dinners for Judy’s birthday and our anniversary.. 

 

As you may recall, nearly four years ago, Judy was injured after being blown over by a strong gust of wind. Despite numerous medical visits that included steroid injections, regenerative medicine, and visits to physical therapy and pain medicine doctors, the injury is still painful and inhibits our activities.  In addition, Judy has developed some cognitive issues that involve memory loss and confusion.  Healthwise, Jerry has the aches and pains one would expect in an octogenarian. He wakes up every morning with some back pain, does about 30 minutes of exercise and is okay until he gets back in bed at night. Before Covid, he went to the gym four or five days a week. He has not resumed that, and it is unlikely, due to Covid, that he will return to the gym in the near future.  He does have an elliptical trainer and does 30 minutes of exercise each day. Since Judy’s injury, Jerry has taken over the culinary responsibilities, and we have delicious vegetarian and pescatarian meals. Photos of some of these have been posted on our Facebook page.

 

 

A highlight of the year was the 30th Anniversary Celebration of Prevention Point on October18th.  Judy was one of the founders of Prevention Point Philadelphia, Philadelphia’s needle exchange.  At this event Judy was presented a special award: The Founders Award for extraordinary work on the front lines of harm reduction.  To mark the occasion, she received the award pictured here.  In addition, in her honor, an award for exemplary service in harm reduction was created in her name.  The initial presentation was this year.  She was pleased that Michael and Don Sapatkin were able to attend the event.

 

Jerry and Judy are cochairs of the Philadelphia section of the Princeton Prize in Race Relations. The PPRR honors high school students who have worked to advance racial equity in their communities. By honoring these students, we encourage others to do this important work. Each year our section honors the local winners at a reception.  This year the reception was held on Sunday, May 15 and we were glad that we were able to attend and meet the remarkable young people that received the awards.

 

Judy does not have much contact with Bryn Mawr but we did attend retirement dinners honoring Bob Washington and Maty Osirim.  It was good to see them and other colleagues.  She very much misses the active life that she formerly enjoyed. Her interpersonal contacts are limited to telephone calls or Facetime. As a member of the Board of Women Against Abuse and a member of the (Philadelphia) Mayor’s Drug & Alcohol Executive Commission, she attends meetings via Zoom.

 

Jerry continues as a pre-major advisor at Penn. Each year he is assigned ten freshman whom he advises, primarily on course selection until they select a major during their second year. Usually, he meets with his advisees twice a year for spring/fall course selection. Due to Covid, this year his advising was done via email despite the fact that Penn held in person classes. Advising is a good way for him to keep in contact with the College and with students.  As a past-president Jerry is a non-voting member of the Council of the Emeritus Association (PASEF). PASEF and the medical school counterpart, ASEF, present interesting lectures which can he views on Zoom.  Jerry is a member of the national board of Americans for Democratic Action.  But his activity is limited to attending board meetings on Zoom and making donations. 

 

The MAA summer meeting (Mathfest) was held in Philadelphia this past summer.

While Jerry attended most sessions virtually, Jerry and Judy were please to attend some receptions in person.

It was a real treat to see friends “face-to-face”.

 

This year, Jerry celebrated his 85th birthday.  He was given a gala party with Rebecca, Steve, Caroline, Mike, Elaine, Henry, Allen and Rhona joining Judy for the event.

 

 

For both, Judy’s birthday and our 62nd anniversary we celebrated at Estia Taverna in Radnor.  Steve and Rebecca joined us for Judy’s birthday.

 

We celebrated the first Passover seder with Mike, Elaine and Henry and our good friend, Ann Weiss.

 

Enough about us! Let’s turn to updates about our children and grandchildren.

We usually end our letter by remembering friends and colleagues who have died since our last letter and we will do this below.  It is staggering, however, to note that according to the New York Times: Russia’s war in Ukraine has left more than 100,000 of Moscow’s troops dead or wounded, and Ukraine has probably suffered a similar number of casualties.  Nearly as many people have died in the US from gun violence and drug overdose.

According to the Gun Violence Archive, which keeps tracks of US mass shootings more than 44,000 people died from gun violence in 2022, and data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention found a staggering 110,236 people died  from drug overdose.  And we should not forget that the COVID epidemic is not over. More than 267,000 people died of Covid-19 in the US in 2022.
